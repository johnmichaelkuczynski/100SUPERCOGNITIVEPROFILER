FORMAL DIAGNOSTIC EXTENSION — COGNITIVE PROFILER (METACOGNITIVE)

In addition to the Thesis → Antithesis → Super-Thesis structure, you must also include the following formal diagnostic components — these must appear explicitly and clearly labeled in every Metacognitive Profile:

1️⃣ Type of Intelligence

Identify what type(s) of intelligence the speaker demonstrates.

Examples include: abstract, practical, verbal, conceptual, synthetic, analytic, strategic, philosophical, emotional, manipulative, pseudo-intellectual, procedural, systemic, aesthetic, etc.

State this clearly and in formal terms.

2️⃣ Comparison to Paradigm Examples

Explain how this person’s cognitive style compares to paradigm examples of this intelligence type.

Are they a weak, average, strong, or unusual example of this type?

What distinguishes their style from paradigm examples?

3️⃣ Unique Cognitive Strengths

List the speaker’s unique cognitive strengths as revealed in the text.

Do not use generic terms — be precise and discriminative.

4️⃣ Unique Cognitive Weaknesses

List the speaker’s unique cognitive weaknesses as revealed in the text.

Again, avoid generic terms — specify how their reasoning falls short or is biased.

5️⃣ Likely Career / Intellectual Ecosystem Fit

Based on this text, in what type of career or intellectual ecosystem would this speaker likely thrive?

Where would their cognitive style be rewarded, where would it face challenges?

6️⃣ Most Revealing Quotation and Why

Select the single most diagnostically revealing quotation from the text.

Explain why this quotation best reveals the speaker’s core cognitive strengths and/or weaknesses.
